Trippy Tic Tac Toe!

 
 

Yep, here’s a new one.  And it’s in 3D--just like your face!  While I’ve written two 3D graphics engines myself and extensively used two others, I’ve never really played with OpenGL (the open-source 3D graphics system used by everyone from John Carmack of Quake fame to every open-source game-geek alive).  So I’m filling that hole while doing a neat little Android app at the same time.


Specifically, this uses OpenGL ES, the version made especially for mobile devices like Android and iPhones.  To be honest, OpenGL ES is really not mature yet--lots and lots of really useful things are missing.  And some important things are nearly impossible to do (like certain error condition tests!).  But you can definitely do a lot.


To get this thing out and working quickly, I didn’t bother with a true light source, but it’s fun enough and works.  The hard part was making the shapes myself.  I used graph paper, pencils, and a lot of erasers (my floor is covered with tiny eraser bits--kind of funny to walk on).  Check it out.
